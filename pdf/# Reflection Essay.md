# Reflection Essay
**Ponpawit Potisom**
/ **Charleston Southern University**
Cybersecurity Major

---

One big challenge I had during my academic journey was the language barrier. English is not my first language, so when I first started college, it took me much longer to read, understand instructions, and complete assignments. This was not a technical problem with computers, but it still affected my technical classes because I had to spend extra time understanding the questions before I could even start coding or solving problems. Sometimes I felt behind because I needed more time than other students.
I overcame this challenge by staying disciplined and managing my time better. I started reading assignments earlier and asking questions plus getting time with my professor when I did not understand something. I also practiced speaking and writing English more every day. Over time, I became more confident. This challenge taught me patience, hard work, and how to not give up when something feels difficult.

---

In one of my classes, I worked on a team project where we had to build a system together. I was the kind of teammate who liked to jump in and help wherever I was needed. If someone was confused, I would try to explain the task or offer support. I care about the success of the whole team, not just my part.
However, I know I can improve my communication skills. Sometimes I focus so much on finishing the work that I forget to clearly explain my ideas or update the team. In the future, I want to work on speaking up more during meetings and checking in with teammates regularly. I want to become not just a helpful teammate, but also a strong communicator.

---

I believe I am most suited for a career in cybersecurity. I enjoy working with computers and learning how systems stay safe. Even though I am not the strongest at coding, I am very interested in protecting information and understanding how attacks happen. Cybersecurity is not only about writing code. It is also about thinking carefully, finding weaknesses, and solving problems.
I chose this field because I like analyzing systems and keeping people safe online. I enjoy learning about networks, security tools, and risk management. This career fits my strengths because I am patient, detail-oriented, and serious about responsibility.

---

In the future, I would like to join professional organizations such as (ISC)² and ISSA. These groups focus on cybersecurity and information systems. They provide training, certifications, and networking opportunities with professionals in the field. Being part of these organizations would help me grow and stay updated on new security threats and technologies. In the next five years, I would like to earn certifications such as Security+ and possibly CISSP. 

---

I can use my computer science skills to help protect people and organizations in my community. For example, I can help small businesses improve their cybersecurity so they do not get hacked. Many small businesses do not have strong protection, so even simple advice like using strong passwords, backing up files, and installing security software can make a big difference. I can help them understand basic security steps that keep their customer information safe.
I also want to use my skills to help churches back in Thailand, where I am originally from. Many churches, especially in Asia. There may not be strong technical support. I can help them protect their online giving systems, member information, and church websites. I can also help them set up secure networks and teach leaders how to avoid phishing emails and scams. Churches often trust people easily, so they can become easy targets for cyber-attacks. By helping them understand online safety, I can protect their mission and the people they serve.
In addition, I can teach young students in Thailand about technology and cybersecurity, especially my family back home. I can show them how to use computers wisely and safely. I believe technology can be a tool to serve others, not just to make money. By using what I have learned in computer science, I can give back to both my local community and my home country. That is important to me because I want my skills to make a real difference in people’s lives.
